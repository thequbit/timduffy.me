2017 Election Day Info

November 7th, 2017 we vote at 60 Erie Station Rd, at the West Henrieta Fire House.

Ballots are notorious for being difficult to understand. If this is your first time voting, hopefully this small description will help you.

There are four different positions that are going to be filled this election day. Those four positions are:

  • State Supreme Court Justice, 7th Judicial District
  • Family Court Judge
  • Sheriff
  • Town Supervisor
  • (2) Members of Town Council

There are 7 different political parties represented on our ballot. Click the links below to get brought to the parties website or facebook page.

  • Democratic
  • Republican
  • Conservative
  • Working Families
  • Independence
  • Reform
  • Bright Future ( no website available )

When filling our the ballot, you should only have one mark per vertical column, since a column represents a single office being filled. In the case of Town Supervisor, there are three unique names that will be on the ballot:

  • Stephen L Schultz ( Democratic and Working Families )
  • Jack W Moore ( Republican, Conservative, Independence, and Reform )
  • Jeffery P Kueppers ( Bright Future )

You may only vote for a single supervisor, so you would bubble-in a single name in the Supervisor column. You may, however, pick which party you'd like to show support for when voting for said candidate.

If you were going to vote for Schultz, you could do so in one of two places: Democratic or Working Families. If you were going to vote for Moore, then you have three options. You have but one option for Kueppers.

In the case of the Member of Town Council, you will be filling out the two columns as a single large column where any two candidates can be selected. That is, of the 5 possible options, you should have only 2 bubbles filled in.
